Now that my first year of grad school is over, I realized there were some things I learned that I wish I had known before I started a PhD program. I thought it would be nice to have a reflection of my first year somewhere, and maybe be able to help someone on their grad journey. <br>

### Tools
#### Zotero
- A citation manager that can also access articles.

#### LaTeX
- A typesetting software system that makes great-looking documents. I'm still learning to use it, but I know there's no going back to Google Docs for my papers. I wish I had started using it sooner because it would have made formatting syntax problem sets so much easier. Plus, the documents it produces are just crisp and professional, and pleasing on the eye. <br>

### Tips
- Drink more water. If you think you're drinking enough, drink more.
- Find something (at least one thing) to do on a regular basis that is not on campus or at home. Maybe this is just me, but during the busy parts of the semester, I sometimes feel like my entire life is grad school, so it can be good to do something elsewhere to reset and refresh your mind. <br>
